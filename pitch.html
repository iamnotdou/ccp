The core insight

The existing playbook is wrong.

Reputation Architecture

Reputation systems assume stable preferences, stable incentives, and stable decision-making. LLM agents violate all three. Model updates silently change the scored entity. Past behavior weakly predicts future behavior. New identities cost nothing to spin up.

The right output is a risk function, not a trust score.

The protocol

Three primitives.

01

Contain

Agent-independent spend limits defined before deployment. Hard structural caps — not soft behavioral guidelines. The model cannot override them.

02

Reserve

Capital locked on-chain in a smart contract to back the containment bound. Real funds, held in escrow, released only on clean certificate expiry.

03

Certify

A certified auditor stakes their own capital alongside yours. Their reputation and funds are slashed if the attestation fails. Skin in the game, on-chain.
